Premise

Rant 11 keeps the short-form side of the mockup feeling populated. By now the main benefit is comparative: the page can be judged against the posts template and against the archive listing that points to it.

That three-way comparison is where mock pages really start paying off. It also becomes easier to test whether a short complaint can support a sidenote without feeling comically over-engineered.It probably can, as long as the sidenote feels like an aside rather than a second essay trying to claw its way onto the page.

One More Thing

Short pages can still carry a visual interruption, provided it arrives cleanly and leaves quickly.

.Lhot_path:
        mov     eax, DWORD PTR [rsp + 12]
        test    eax, eax
        jle     .Lcold_path
        imul    eax, eax, 5
        ret
.Lcold_path:
        xor     eax, eax
        ret

That kind of mismatch is worth testing in a mockup. Real sites rarely stay inside their original assumptions for long.
